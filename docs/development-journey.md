# Development Journey

My career began in software development roles within banking and enterprise systems. This phase built my engineering foundation in system behavior, structured programming, production support, and business flow implementation.

## Early Foundation
I worked on enterprise systems where stability, correctness, and business continuity were critical. My responsibilities included coding, analyzing existing applications, participating in design reviews, and supporting production and batch operations.

## What I learned from development
- How enterprise systems behave in real production conditions
- How backend logic, jobs, and interfaces affect business outcomes
- How to diagnose failures using logs, job outputs, and runtime behavior
- How design decisions influence maintainability and testability
- How to think in terms of flows, dependencies, and downstream effects

## How this helped later
This development experience became a major advantage in automation and quality engineering. It improved my ability to:
- build stable, engineering-led automation frameworks
- understand integration-heavy systems more deeply
- validate backend and batch workflows better
- communicate effectively with developers and architects
- approach quality as a software engineering discipline
